## 7. Strategic Recommendations

### 7.1 Our Core Competitive Moat

Our platform's strongest competitive advantage is the **native integration with HR operational data**. No standalone wellbeing vendor can match:
- Real overtime hours (not self-reported) correlated with burnout scores
- Sick leave patterns triggering automated wellbeing referrals
- Project workload data enriching daily pulse analysis
- Housing conditions correlated with mental health metrics
- Conflict/complaint data linked to team wellbeing trends

**Recommendation:** Double down on this advantage. Every new feature should leverage our unique data position. For example, our AI intervention matching (P5) would outperform Spring Health's because we have 10x more data signals.

### 7.2 Biggest Vulnerability

Our biggest gap is the **content and self-service layer**. Employees who open Headspace or Calm get immediate value (a meditation, a sleep story). Employees who open our platform without an active issue have limited engagement options.

**Recommendation:** Address P1 (micro-exercises) and P9 (meditation content) to create a "daily value" loop. Consider content partnership rather than building from scratch.

### 7.3 Market Positioning

Position as: **"The only wellbeing platform that knows when your employees need help before they ask."**

This differentiates from:
- Headspace/Calm = "We give employees tools" (reactive self-service)
- Lyra/Spring/Modern Health = "We connect employees to therapists" (reactive clinical)
- Personify/Unmind = "We measure population health" (aggregate analytics)
- **Our Platform** = "We detect risk from real work data and intervene proactively" (proactive prevention)
