**We apply the same principle to workforce stability.** Our "vital signs" are mood, stress, sleep, workload, housing quality, overtime, sick leave, and conflict. # Chapter 4: Housing Stability Index (HSI)

## 4.1 The Seven Dimensions

The Housing Stability Index measures seven dimensions of accommodation quality, each backed by academic evidence linking it to employee wellbeing:

| # | Dimension | Weight | Evidence | Measurement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Privacy** | Very High | Strongest mental health predictor (Evans, 2003) | Single vs. shared room, door locks |
| 2 | **Room Cleanliness** | High | Direct mood correlation (our data) | 1-10 inspection score |
| 3 | **Common Areas** | High | Social conflict predictor | 1-10 inspection score |
| 4 | **Bathroom** | Medium | Hygiene stress indicator | 1-10 inspection score |
| 5 | **Kitchen** | Medium | Nutritional wellbeing factor | 1-10 inspection score |
| 6 | **Safety** | High | Strong anxiety predictor | Locks, lighting, fire safety |
| 7 | **Noise** | Medium | Sleep disruption, cortisol elevation | Subjective rating + decibel check |

### Scoring Methodology

The overall HSI is calculated as a weighted average:

```
HSI = (Room × 1.2 + Common × 1.0 + Bathroom × 0.9 + Kitchen × 0.9) / 4.0
```

Higher weight on room cleanliness reflects its stronger correlation with individual wellbeing outcomes.

### Categorization

| Category | HSI Score | Typical Wellbeing Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Clean** | ≥8.0 | Positive: supports good sleep, low stress |
| **Average** | 5.0-7.9 | Neutral: no significant wellbeing impact |
| **Poor** | <5.0 | Negative: sleep disruption, elevated stress, mood decline |

# Chapter 5: WellMind — The Preventive Wellbeing Engine

## 5.1 Daily Pulse Surveys

The foundation of WellMind is the daily pulse survey — a **30-second mood check-in** that captures four dimensions every day:

1. **Mood** (1-5 emoji scale): 😞 😕 😐 🙂 😁
2. **Stress level** (1-10): "How stressful is your day?"
3. **Sleep quality** (1-10): "How was your sleep last night?"
4. **Workload** (1-10): "How manageable is your current workload?"

Plus an optional free-text note and weekly rotation questions covering housing satisfaction, recognition, safety, work-life balance, conflict, resources, clarity, and team connection.

### Why Daily Matters

Annual surveys capture a snapshot. Monthly surveys capture trends. But **daily surveys capture the micro-longitudinal data** needed for early detection. A single bad day means nothing. Three consecutive bad days is a signal. Five consecutive bad days is an alert.

The 7-day moving average smooths daily variation while remaining sensitive to genuine trends. Anomaly detection fires when the current score drops more than 2 points below the 7-day average.

## 5.2 Question Rotation

Survey fatigue is the enemy of sustained participation. The platform combats this with **weekly question rotation**:

- **Core questions** (always present): Mood, stress, sleep, workload
- **Rotation pool** (8 additional questions, 1-2 shown per week):
  - Housing satisfaction
  - Recognition at work
  - Workplace safety
  - Work-life balance
  - Conflict experience
  - Resource availability
  - Role clarity
  - Team connection

The rotation is deterministic per user per week (seeded by user ID + week number), ensuring the same questions appear for the same user throughout the week but rotate across weeks.

## 5.5 NLP Sentiment Analysis

The platform offers AI-powered analysis of free-text pulse survey notes using Claude (Anthropic):

- **Opt-in only**: Disabled by default, requires explicit employee consent
- **No emotion inference**: Only analyzes text the employee voluntarily wrote (EU AI Act Article 5(1)(f) compliant)
- **Crisis detection**: Identifies language patterns associated with self-harm, severe distress, or crisis
- **Auto-escalation**: Crisis-level notes trigger immediate CarePath referral + HR notification
- **Privacy**: Notes are analyzed via API call, not stored by the AI provider (confirmed via DPA)

## 5.6 Quarterly Assessments (MBI-GS + UWES-9)

Every quarter, employees complete two clinically validated instruments:

**Maslach Burnout Inventory — General Survey (MBI-GS)**:
- 16 items measuring three dimensions:
  - Emotional Exhaustion (EE): "I feel emotionally drained from my work"
  - Cynicism (CY): "I have become less enthusiastic about my work"
  - Professional Efficacy (PE): "I can effectively solve the problems that arise in my work"
- Scoring: 0 (never) to 6 (every day), mean per subscale
- High burnout: EE >2.9, CY >2.2, PE <3.49

**Utrecht Work Engagement Scale (UWES-9)**:
- 9 items measuring three dimensions:
  - Vigor: "At my work, I feel bursting with energy"
  - Dedication: "I am enthusiastic about my job"
  - Absorption: "I am immersed in my work"
- Scoring: 0 (never) to 6 (always), total mean
- Low engagement: total <2.88

|---|---|---|---|
| 🟢 Green | <50% | >60% | No intervention needed |
| 🟡 Yellow | 50-70% | 40-60% | Monitoring + recommendations |

# Chapter 8: GDPR Deep Dive

## 8.1 Health Data Under Article 9

Employee wellbeing data — mood scores, burnout assessments, EAP cases, coaching session notes — qualifies as **health data** under GDPR Article 9. This is "special category data" that is **prohibited from processing** unless specific exceptions apply.

### Applicable Legal Bases

| Legal Basis | GDPR Article | Application in Our Platform |
|---|---|---|
| **Explicit consent** | 9(2)(a) | Per-data-type opt-in via Consent Management UI |
| **Employment obligation** | 9(2)(b) | Hungarian Labour Code §166 (healthy working conditions) |
| **Occupational health** | 9(2)(h) | Wellbeing monitoring under health professional responsibility |

### Key Requirements

| Requirement | Our Implementation |
|---|---|
| **Purpose limitation** | Data used exclusively for employee wellbeing — never for performance evaluation, hiring, or discipline |
| **Data minimization** | Only 4 core fields (mood, stress, sleep, workload) + optional notes |
| **Consent withdrawable** | Any consent can be withdrawn at any time without negative consequences |
| **Right to erasure** | Soft delete (30-day grace period) → hard delete via cron job |
| **Right to portability** | JSON export endpoint (`GET /api/v1/user/export-data`) |
| **DPIA mandatory** | 19-page DPIA document completed (2 of 3 Article 35 criteria met) |

## 18.4 Lessons Learned from Early Adopters

### Lesson 1: Start with Housing
Companies that began their wellbeing program by improving housing conditions saw **3x faster adoption** of digital wellbeing tools. When basic needs are met, employees are more receptive to self-reporting.

### Lesson 2: Gamification Drives Adoption
The introduction of points, badges, and streaks consistently increased daily pulse participation by **30-40%** across all pilot sites. The most effective element was the streak counter — employees didn't want to "break their streak."

### Lesson 3: Manager Training is Non-Negotiable
Without the CARE conversation framework training, managers either ignored wellbeing trends or over-reacted. The 2-hour training investment pays for itself within the first month.

### Lesson 4: Anonymous Options Build Trust
Making CarePath cases anonymous increased initial utilization from **5% to 18%** in the first quarter. After trust was established, 60% of new cases were filed non-anonymously.

### Lesson 5: Integration Beats Standalone
Connecting wellbeing data to HR operational data (overtime, sick leave, housing) created insights impossible to achieve with standalone wellbeing platforms. The **cross-module correlation** is our strongest competitive advantage.

```
Platform annual cost = Employees × Price × 12
                     = 200 × €6 × 12
                     = €14,400

    = €573,000 / €14,400
    = 39.8x
```
